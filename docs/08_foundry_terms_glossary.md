# Foundry Terms Glossary

## Glossary

| Term | Plain English | Industrial Importance | Impact on Casting Quality |
|---|---|---|---|
| Fleet | The full group of batches/castings in the uploaded file. | Used for overall quality and stability summaries. | Shows whether risk is isolated or widespread. |
| Heat | One melt or furnace batch of metal. | Basic production unit in melting. | Heat chemistry and temperature determine casting quality. |
| Batch | A row or production unit being analyzed. | Used interchangeably with heat/casting depending on dataset. | Each batch receives prediction and risk decision. |
| Casting | Final solid metal part produced from molten metal. | Output of the foundry process. | Defects reduce usability, machining yield, and customer acceptance. |
| Tapping Temp | Temperature when molten metal is tapped from furnace. | Starting temperature before transfer and treatment. | Too low causes poor filling; too high increases oxidation. |
| Pouring Temp | Temperature when metal is poured into mold. | Directly affects filling and solidification. | Low causes cold shuts/misruns; high causes oxidation/gas/coarse structure. |
| Temperature Loss | Tapping temperature minus pouring temperature. | Indicates ladle transfer heat loss. | High loss causes cold pour, poor filling, and process instability. |
| Mg Recovery | Fraction of added magnesium retained in metal. | Critical for SG iron nodularization. | Low recovery causes flake or vermicular graphite and poor nodularity. |
| Nodularity | Degree to which graphite forms as nodules rather than flakes. | Key quality property of ductile/SG iron. | Poor nodularity reduces strength and ductility. |
| CE | Carbon Equivalent, usually `C + (Si + P)/3`. | Predicts cast iron solidification behavior. | Low CE can cause shrinkage/hard spots; high CE can cause graphite flotation. |
| Inoculation | Addition of inoculants to promote graphite nucleation. | Controls graphite structure and chill tendency. | Poor inoculation causes hard spots, chill, and graphite issues. |
| Shrinkage | Void caused by contraction during solidification. | Common internal casting defect. | Leads to leakage, weakness, rejection, or machining exposure. |
| Gas Defect | Pores caused by trapped gas such as hydrogen/nitrogen or Mg vapor. | Common defect in castings. | Causes pinholes, blowholes, leakage, and surface/internal rejection. |
| Pigging | Foundry term often related to pig iron handling/addition or process coding. | May appear as traceability/process field. | Can influence chemistry depending on material quality. |
| Heel Metal | Residual molten metal left in furnace between heats. | Improves continuity but carries impurities. | High heel can accumulate sulfur, nitrogen, and trace elements. |
| FSM | Ferro Silicon Magnesium. | Nodularizer used in SG iron treatment. | Incorrect dose affects Mg recovery and nodularity. |
| Barinoc | Inoculant product/material name. | Supports nucleation and graphite control. | Inadequate use can increase chill or poor graphite structure. |
| Reseed | Inoculant product/material name. | Used to improve nucleation. | Helps reduce chill and stabilize graphite formation. |
| Ultraseed | Inoculant product/material name. | Advanced inoculation material. | Improves graphite nucleation and uniformity. |
| Superseed | Inoculant product/material name. | Inoculation material for graphite control. | Affects chill, nodule count, and machinability. |
| RR | May refer to returns/revert ratio depending on plant terminology. | Important if it indicates return scrap in charge. | High return content can affect chemistry stability. |
| Sorel | High-purity pig iron brand/material. | Charge material with controlled chemistry. | Helps stabilize base iron quality. |
| CRCA | Cold Rolled Close Annealed scrap. | Scrap/charge material that may carry moisture or coatings. | Moisture/coatings can raise gas porosity risk. |
| Sulfur | Element S in iron. | Consumes magnesium and harms nodularization. | High sulfur causes poor nodularity and hot shortness. |
| Manganese | Element Mn. | Neutralizes sulfur by forming MnS. | Too low Mn/S ratio leaves sulfur active; too high Mn can promote carbides. |
| Mn/S Ratio | Manganese divided by sulfur. | Indicates sulfur neutralization capacity. | Low ratio increases sulfur-related defects. |
| Silicon | Element Si. | Graphitizer and inoculation-related chemistry element. | Low Si increases shrinkage/chill; high Si affects properties. |
| Carbon | Element C. | Main cast iron chemistry component. | Controls CE, graphite, shrinkage, and hardness. |
| Phosphorus | Element P. | Part of CE formula; affects fluidity and brittleness. | High P can reduce toughness and cause embrittlement. |
| Magnesium | Element Mg. | Required for spheroidal graphite in SG iron. | Too little causes flake graphite; too much may cause gas/dross. |
| Nitrogen | Element N. | Gas-forming element. | High N can create blowholes/pinholes. |
| Aluminium | Element Al. | Strong oxide former. | High Al can cause pinholes or oxide inclusions. |
| Chromium | Element Cr. | Carbide stabilizer. | High Cr can cause white iron/hard spots. |
| Molybdenum | Element Mo. | Carbide/pearlite stabilizing alloy. | Can increase hardness and carbide tendency. |
| White Iron | Iron structure with carbides instead of graphite. | Hard and brittle structure. | Causes hard spots and poor machinability. |
| Cold Shut | Defect where streams of metal fail to fuse. | Usually caused by low temperature or poor flow. | Surface defect and structural weakness. |
| Misrun | Incomplete mold filling. | Caused by low temperature or poor fluidity. | Casting shape incomplete, usually rejected. |
| Graphite Flotation | Graphite rises/separates in high-carbon conditions. | Risk in high CE or thick sections. | Causes poor surface/internal quality. |
| Vermicular Graphite | Worm-like graphite form between flake and nodular. | Indicates poor Mg treatment control. | Reduces expected SG iron properties. |
| Flake Graphite | Graphite in flake shape. | Normal for gray iron but bad for SG iron when nodules expected. | Reduces ductility in ductile iron. |
| Nodule Count | Number of graphite nodules per area. | Indicates inoculation/nodularization quality. | Low count can reduce mechanical properties. |
| Charge Material | Scrap, pig iron, returns, and additions loaded into furnace. | Determines base chemistry and impurities. | Poor charge control causes chemistry variation. |
| Ladle | Vessel used to transfer molten metal. | Affects heat loss and treatment. | Poor preheat/insulation causes temperature loss. |
| Desulfurization | Removing sulfur from molten iron. | Improves Mg treatment efficiency. | Reduces nodularity failure risk. |
| Treatment | Addition process, often Mg treatment for SG iron. | Converts graphite shape to nodular. | Poor treatment creates flake/vermicular graphite. |
| Re-treatment | Additional correction treatment after poor initial result. | Used for recoverable heats. | Can reduce HOLD risk if done correctly. |
